Summer Solstice

BY GARRETT HOTTLE - Saturday, June 20th, 2020 - Well you can now officially tell your friends it's summer, following today's summer solstice which occurred before 4 p.m. today... Sunset will come around 9 p.m. this evening, and we'll see an increase in cloud cover as we move forward in both the Magic Valley and Wood River Valley. Overnight lows will land in the 50s in both regions and it will be on the breezier side of the things in the Wood River Valley where gusts up to 25 mph will be possible.

For Father's Day tomorrow expect mostly sunny skies in the Magic Valley and Wood River Valley with cooler temperatures and breezy conditions in both regions. Highs will land in the low 80s in the Magic Valley, and mid and upper 70s in the Wood River Valley. Sustained winds between 10-20 mph and gusts as strong as 25 mph will be expected.

...Mostly sunny skies are expected through at least mid week Wednesday, which appears to be the warmest day of the week in our area. Expect highs to land in the lower 90s in the Magic Valley and mid 80s in the Wood River Valley. KMTV-11
