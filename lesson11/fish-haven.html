The First Day of Summer

EVERYTHING YOU SHOULD KNOW ABOUT THE LONGEST DAY OF THE YEAR - by Farmer's Almanac - In 2020, the June solstice occurs on Saturday, June 20, marking the start of summer in the Northern Hemisphere. The June solstice (aka summer solstice) occurs when the Sun reaches its highest and northernmost points in the sky. This event marks the start of summer in the northern half of the globe.

WHEN IS SUMMER SOLTICE? In 2020, the June solstice is Saturday, June 20, at 5:44 P.M. EDT. This date marks the official beginning of summer in the Northern Hemisphere, occurring when Earth arrives at the point in its orbit where the North Pole is at its maximum tilt toward the Sun, resulting in the longest day and shortest night of the calendar year.

Also, an annular solar eclipse will occur just before midnight (EDT) on June 20, reaching its maximum point at 2:40 AM EDT on the 21st!
