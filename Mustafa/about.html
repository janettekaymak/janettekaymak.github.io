Mustafa Kaymak is a Turkish/Kurdish playwright and film producer based in the United States. He was born in Ankara, Turkey on July 24 1984. After graduating from Ankara University’s Communication Faculty with a Journalism Degree in 2008, Kaymak immigrated to Alaska and begin studying English. While at Prince William Sound Community College, in beautiful Valdez AK, he fell in love with theater and decided to pursue a master's degree. In 2011, Kaymak moved to New York City to begin his MFA in Playwriting at Columbia University. Upon finishing his degree, he began his second MFA in Creative Film Producing, also at Columbia. Kaymak’s thesis film "Green" won the short film Jury Award at Sundance Film Festival in 2019.

Mustafa's early childhood was spent in a small village outside of Ankara called Altilar. It was settled by his ancestors and Mustafa spent many happy hours playing with his extensive extended family in the orchard and garden. He tended sheep with the shepherds and helped on his Grandpa's farm, harvesting cumin. He loved the Anatolian Sheep dogs, of which the village had many. When his father became ill with MS, they moved to Ankara. His father died when Mustafa was nine years old. He is the youngest of five children.

Mustafa and his wife Janette (nee) Hasson have been married since 2008 and they live in Upper Westside Manhattan. They have an adorable Morkie dog named Neşe (pronounced Neh-sheh), which is a Turkish word for 'cheerful'. Janette was born and raised in the Western United States and has six children from a previous marriage. She is his Script Editor and one of his Dramaturgs. Janette is also a Web Designer and Developer and created this website.
